# Nav-IR concepts

This page describes the route/segment/geometry model and how it maps to common notions (leg, step, maneuver).

## Route

Top-level container for a navigation route. It has:

- **id** – Unique route identifier (UUID).
- **metadata** – Name, description, timestamps, optional total distance/duration, tags. Informational only; does not affect execution.
- **segments** – One or more route segments (see below). Order is significant.
- **policies** – Off-route behavior (recalculate, snap, or alert) and snapping mode. Apply to the whole route.

A valid route has at least one segment.

## Segment (≈ leg)

A **segment** is the unit of intent and geometry. In other ecosystems this is often called a "leg."

- **Intent** – `FixedGeometry` (e.g. imported track), `Recalculatable` (e.g. engine route), or `AdvisoryTrack`. Drives whether the runtime may recalculate or must follow the geometry.
- **Geometry** – One encoded polyline plus source and confidence (see Geometry).
- **Waypoints** – Ordered list of start, via, stop (and optional shaping/POI). At least start and stop in practice.
- **Instructions** – Optional turn/arrive/depart steps along the segment (see Instructions).
- **Constraints** – Routing hints (allow_reroute, avoid_*, etc.). Inform engines; do not mandate behavior.

Segments allow mixed-mode routes, recalculation boundaries, and multi-day trips (e.g. one segment per day or per mode).

## Waypoints

Points the user cares about along the route:

- **Coordinate** – Latitude and longitude.
- **Kind** – `Start`, `Stop`, `Via`, `Shaping`, `Poi`, `Fuel`, `Break`.
- **radius_m** – Optional arrival/trigger radius in meters.

Waypoints are ordered within a segment. Start and Stop typically bookend the segment.

## Instructions (≈ step / maneuver)

Optional turn-by-turn or semantic steps along the segment:

- **Coordinate** – Where the instruction applies.
- **Kind** – `TurnLeft`, `TurnRight`, `Continue`, `Arrive`, `Depart`, `Merge`, `Roundabout`.
- **distance_to_next_m** – Optional distance to the next instruction.
- **street_name** – Optional road name.

Instructions may be imported (e.g. from a file) or generated by a routing engine. They are optional for execution; geometry and waypoints are sufficient for basic following.

## Geometry

Each segment has one geometry:

- **polyline** – Encoded polyline string (e.g. Google polyline encoding).
- **source** – `ImportedExact` (e.g. GPX), `SnappedToGraph` (e.g. OSRM), `Recalculated`, or `Synthetic`.
- **confidence** – `High`, `Medium`, or `Low`. Geometry is authoritative only when high confidence and imported exact.
- **bounding_box** – Min/max lat/lon for the segment.

## Intent and policies

- **SegmentIntent** – Per segment: fixed geometry (do not recalc), recalculatable (engine may recalc), or advisory (suggested track).
- **RoutePolicies** – For the whole route: what to do when off-route (`Recalculate`, `SnapToRoute`, `AlertOnly`) and how strict snapping is (`Strict`, `Relaxed`, `Off`).

Execution state (current position, visited waypoints, ETA) is **separate** from Nav-IR. Nav-IR describes the route contract; the core or device maintains execution state.
